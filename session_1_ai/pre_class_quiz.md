# Pre-Class Warmup: The Economics of AI

*Instructions: These questions are designed to get you thinking about the economic impact of AI before we dive into the theory. There are no "wrong" answers at this stage, but try to use your economic intuition.*

## Question 1: The Core Mechanism
When ChatGPT writes an essay or Netflix recommends a movie, what is it primarily doing from a mathematical/economic perspective?
A. It is "understanding" the concepts like a human.
B. It is performing a low-cost **prediction** (predicting the next word, or your likely rating) based on past data.
C. It is accessing a fixed database of facts.
D. It is generating random noise.

## Question 2: Historical Comparison
The Industrial Revolution replaced human **physical muscle** with steam and electric power. What is the fundamental shift offered by the current AI revolution?
A. It replaces physical movement with robots.
B. It replaces **cognitive and creative labor** (prediction, generation, reasoning) with cheap compute.
C. It reduces the need for energy.
D. It increases the cost of information.

## Question 3: Winner-Takes-All
