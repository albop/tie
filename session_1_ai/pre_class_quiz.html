Pre-Class Warmup: The Economics of AI

Instructions: These questions are designed to get you thinking about the economic impact of AI before we dive into the theory. There are no “wrong” answers at this stage, but try to use your economic intuition.

Question 1: The Core Mechanism

When ChatGPT writes an essay or Netflix recommends a movie, what is it primarily doing from a mathematical/economic perspective? A. It is “understanding” the concepts like a human. B. It is performing a low-cost prediction (predicting the next word, or your likely rating) based on past data. C. It is accessing a fixed database of facts. D. It is generating random noise.

Question 2: Historical Comparison

The Industrial Revolution replaced human physical muscle with steam and electric power. What is the fundamental shift offered by the current AI revolution? A. It replaces physical movement with robots. B. It replaces cognitive and creative labor (prediction, generation, reasoning) with cheap compute. C. It reduces the need for energy. D. It increases the cost of information.

Question 3: Winner-Takes-All

Why are AI markets (like Search or LLMs) often dominated by one or two giant firms? A. Because they bribe the government. B. Because of Data Network Effects: More users \(\to\) More Data \(\to\) Better Product \(\to\) More Users. C. Because these companies existed before the Internet. D. Because AI requires no capital investment.

Question 4: Open Reflection (Short Response)

Think of the job you want to have in 5 years. Name ONE specific task in that job that you believe AI will NEVER be able to do well.

(Max 1 sentence)

Question 5: A Language Riddle (Pattern Recognition)

What occurs once in a minute, twice in a moment, but never in a thousand years? (Hint: Don’t think about time. Look closer at the words themselves.)

A. A thought B. The letter ‘M’ C. A second D. The present

Correct Answer: B (This tests your ability to process data (characters) rather than just semantic meaning—much like how an LLM tokenizes text!)
